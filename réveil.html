Réveil

Aubade: various modes by which the amorous subject finds upon waking that he is once again beseiged by the anxieties of his passion.

I woke up in a puddle of sweat
under a pie of sticks
my heart skidded down the hill
my heels are raw, I can't eat

it never stopped
(flip a coin)

Roland Barthes, "A Lover's Discourse," 203.
